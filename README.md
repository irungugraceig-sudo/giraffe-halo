## The Problem

At up to 5.8 meters tall, giraffes are the tallest — and most exposed — land animals on Earth. During storms, they can't fit under trees, can't lie down without risking predators, and can't outrun lightning. They are **30× more likely** to be struck by lightning than humans.

Their only strategy? Stand still and hope.

## The Solution

The **Giraffe HALO** is an autonomous hover ring that follows giraffes during storms, creating a protective zone — without ever touching, disturbing, or even being heard by the animal.

No collar. No harness. No contact. Just a silent ring of protection from above.

---

## How It Works

| System | What It Does | How |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| 🌀 **Counter-Thrust Stability** | Stays locked in position during 95km/h winds | Ring shape = low drag. 6 EHD thrusters fire into the wind 200×/sec via gyroscopic IMU |
| 💨 **Ionic Air Curtain** | Diverts rain and wind around the giraffe | Electrohydrodynamic emitters create an invisible cylinder of fast-moving air |
| ⚡ **Plasma Lightning Intercept** | Attracts strikes to the ring, not the giraffe | UV laser ionizes air above HALO into a conductive plasma channel. Current discharges through ground cables |
| 🔇 **Silent Operation** | Zero disturbance to the animal | EHD propulsion = no moving parts = 12dB (below savanna wind ambient). Auto-retreats if stress detected |

## Origin Story

This project was inspired by a viral video of giraffes standing in a storm at Kenya's Maasai Mara Game Reserve. While LinkedIn turned it into leadership metaphors, we turned it into... whatever this is.

I grew up in Kenya and never once thought about giraffes having nowhere to hide during storms. Ashamed to say it. But the stillness in that video hit different — and somewhere between the genuine reflection and the dark humor, the Giraffe HALO was born.
